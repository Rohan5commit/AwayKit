# Judging Guide

## Why AwayKit Scores Strongly

### Platform Fit (QVAC + Pears + WDK)

AwayKit is the strongest differentiated fit for the hackathon because it uses all three Tether technologies in a single product, each serving a distinct and necessary role:

- **QVAC** powers intelligent local features without cloud dependency
- **Pears** enables group coordination without servers
- **WDK** handles real financial flows with self-custody

### Usefulness

This solves a real problem. Football fans coordinating away trips currently juggle WhatsApp, Splitwise, Google Translate, and maps. AwayKit unifies this into one workflow.

### Privacy

Every screen reinforces the privacy story:
- AI responses show "Powered by QVAC — runs locally"
- Sync status shows peer connections via Pears
- Wallet shows self-custodial balance

### Product Completeness

The demo covers a complete workflow:
1. Create group → 2. Join via P2P → 3. Plan trip → 4. Get AI help → 5. Split expenses → 6. Settle in USDt

### Differentiation

Less crowded than prediction markets and betting clones. Unique football-native approach that hasn't been done before.

## Key Judge Questions

**Q: Why not use cloud AI?**
A: Stadium connectivity is unreliable. Local AI ensures the app works when it matters most.

**Q: Why P2P instead of a server?**
A: No central point of failure, no data collection, works offline, and scales naturally.

**Q: Why self-custodial USDt?**
A: No trusted organizer needed. Direct wallet-to-wallet settlements are trustless and transparent.

**Q: What makes this football-native?**
A: Matchday checklists, away-fan translations, venue guidance, and group fund management — all built around the matchday experience.
